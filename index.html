Reader picks

Roselie's favorite books

  • The Alchemist

    This book is one of my utmost favorites because the author creates a storyline that makes the reader feel that the journey and realizations are their own. It is about a young shepard, who travels to the pyramids of Egypt in search of a treasure he has recurrently dreamed about. He has to overcome many obstacles along the way through which he learns valuable life lessons.

    Paulo Coelho

  • A New Earth

    A piece that will challenge the readers current default state of mind. In this book Tolle expands on powerful ideations about our ego-based state of consciousness and its affect on our suffering. He describes how our attachment to the ego creates dysfunction that leads to anger, jealousy, and unhappiness, and shows the readers how to awaken to a new state of consciousness and truly fulfilling existence.

    Eckhart Tolle

  • Grit: The Power of Passion and Perserverance

    A book to keep one motivated to stay course to their goals and dreams. The secret to outstanding achievement is not talent but grit: a special blend of passion and persistence. Grit is about having a passion and perserverance for long-term goals. Gritty people are able to maintain their determination and motivation over long periods despite experiences with failure and adversity.

    Angela Duckworth

  • The Invincible Company

    This book is for those who aim to be leaders in business. Packed with real-life examples, the reader will be equipped with the blueprint to build a resilient corporate culture that embraces both innovation and reinventation, ensuring the organizations long-term success.

    Dr. Alexander Osterwalder

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and the Triple Peaks Library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 monthly visitors. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and using our ample online resources.

Become a member today, and make sure to check out our Reader Picks section. This week's selection is full of books anyone interested in computer programming would love!
